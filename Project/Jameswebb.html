Some popular universe paradoxes:

Olbers' Paradox:
This paradox questions the darkness of the night sky. It ponders why, given an infinite and eternal universe filled with stars, the night sky isn't completely bright. The paradox suggests that if the universe were infinite and uniformly filled with stars, the sky should be ablaze with light.
The Bootstrap Paradox:
This paradox involves information or objects that have no discernible origin. It occurs when an object or information is brought back in time and becomes its own cause. Essentially, it creates a loop where the origin or creation of the object is unknown.
The Grandfather Paradox:
This paradox explores the implications of time travel. It suggests that if someone were to travel back in time and kill their own grandfather before their parent was conceived, it would create a contradiction, as their existence would be impossible, yet they would have been able to travel back in time.
